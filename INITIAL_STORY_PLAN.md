# APHELION

**The Ship**
The Aphelion is a generation ship carrying its 2nd or 3rd generation population on a journey so long that the original mission parameters have been lost or mythologized. The crew simply continues forward because stopping is unthinkable and there is nothing to return to. The name Aphelion - meaning the point farthest from the sun, maximum cold, maximum distance, never returning - has lost its meaning entirely over generations. It is just a word now. Just home. The protagonist's discovery of what the name actually means is a late pivotal moment that recontextualizes everything and connects directly to the central reveal.

**The Hidden Truth**
Someone on the ship possesses or has recently received information suggesting the original destination was never what the founders claimed. This knowledge is actively suppressed, possibly by people who genuinely believe they're protecting the population rather than acting as outright villains. The moral ambiguity is intentional.

**The Virus**
A virus is spreading that causes hallucinations. Its origin is unexplained until very late in the story. When the origin is finally revealed it connects directly to the suppressed truth about the destination and the meaning of the ship's name - one revelation doing double duty, possibly triple duty. The reveal does not resolve the dread but makes everything worse and more hopeless, creating urgency and suspense rather than relief or comfort.

**Characters**
The protagonist is a woman in her twenties or thirties. Her mother is the other central character, making the emotional core a mother-daughter relationship. Both were born into this life with no choice, inheriting someone else's mission and someone else's secrets. There are other crew and population members aboard - this is not an isolated two-person story. Character names are yet to be developed.

**Tone and Influences**
Very dark. Inspired by the quiet civilizational dissolution, unreliable reality, and two-woman dynamic of Midnight Sun (Twilight Zone) and the feeling of familiar environment becoming alien and unrecognizable from Masks (TNG). Not a possession story. The horror lives in perception, inherited powerlessness, and suppressed truth rather than supernatural agency.
