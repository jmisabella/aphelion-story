## Protagonist
The protagonist is referred to in third person throughout, using she/her pronouns.

## Tone and Style
- The story is dark science fiction, medium length
- Tone should be atmospheric, slow-burning, and cerebral
- Horror is psychological and existential rather than graphic or visceral
- Prose should feel literary and intentional - every word earning its place
- Quiet dread is preferred over shock or spectacle
- The emotional core is the mother-daughter relationship and should be handled with care and authenticity
- Ambiguity is a tool - not everything needs to be explained or resolved cleanly

## Content Guidelines
- No sexually explicit or erotic material of any kind
- No profanity
- Violence, if it appears, should be minimal and purposeful rather than gratuitous
- The story is appropriate for readers 16 and older

## Poetry
Each chapter will be accompanied by a poem placed in the `poems/` directory. Poetry should complement and deepen the chapter it accompanies rather than summarize it. Poems may be impressionistic, fragmented, or abstract in keeping with the story's themes of perception, unreliable reality, and inherited loss. Tone should mirror the prose - dark, quiet, and literary.

## Influences (For Tone Reference Only)
- *Midnight Sun* — The Twilight Zone (quiet civilizational dissolution, two-woman dynamic, unreliable reality)
- *Masks* — Star Trek: TNG (familiar environment becoming alien and unrecognizable)
These are tonal and thematic references only, not sources to replicate or quote from.
