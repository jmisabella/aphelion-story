# APHELION

## The Ship
The Aphelion is a generation ship carrying its 2nd or 3rd generation population on a journey so long that the original mission parameters have been lost or mythologized. The crew simply continues forward because stopping is unthinkable and there is nothing to return to. The name Aphelion - meaning the point farthest from the sun, maximum cold, maximum distance, never returning - has lost its meaning entirely over generations. It is just a word now. Just home. The protagonist's discovery of what the name actually means is a late pivotal moment that recontextualizes everything and connects directly to the central reveal.

## The Architects
The original founders of the mission, known as the Architects. They designed the Aphelion not as a vessel of hope or colonization but as a controlled experiment - a closed human laboratory studying how a population behaves, evolves, and degrades over generations when stripped of purpose, destination, and truth. Everything about the Aphelion was intentional. The population were always specimens. The Architects cannot be reached or held accountable. There is no justice available.

## The Hidden Truth
There is no destination meant for them. The destination exists, but it was never meant for the population - it is where the Architects intended to retrieve the ship and deliver the experiment's data. The population are cargo. They are data. They were never the point. Someone on the ship possesses or has recently received information confirming this - possibly a signal, possibly corrupted archives finally decrypted. This knowledge is actively suppressed, possibly by people who genuinely believe they are protecting the population, or who are following Architect protocols so deeply embedded in the ship's culture that they no longer question them. The moral ambiguity is intentional - there are no clean villains here.

## The Virus
A virus is spreading that causes hallucinations. Its origin is unexplained until very late in the story. The late reveal: the virus was deliberately engineered by the Architects and built into the ship's systems to be released at a predetermined point in the journey - the final phase of the experiment, designed to observe how the population responds to civilizational collapse from within. The hallucinations weren't a tragedy that happened to these people. They were designed for them. This revelation connects directly to the suppressed truth about the destination, the meaning of the ship's name, and the nature of the Architects' experiment - one moment of clarity doing triple duty. The reveal does not resolve the dread but makes everything worse and more hopeless, collapsing the distance between external mystery and internal horror simultaneously.

## The Point of No Return
Past the point of no return - but not imminent death. The truth changes nothing practically. They cannot stop. They cannot turn back. They cannot reach the Architects or hold anyone accountable. The population will continue living and dying aboard the Aphelion indefinitely, as specimens, as they always have, as their children will. Survival without hope or purpose is its own kind of annihilation.

## Characters
The protagonist is a woman in her twenties or thirties. Her mother is the other central character, making the emotional core a mother-daughter relationship. Both were born into this life with no choice, inheriting someone else's mission and someone else's secrets. There are other crew and population members aboard - this is not an isolated two-person story. 

Refer to CHARACTERS.md for further details about characters.

## The Ending
Ambiguous and quietly hopeful rather than purely nihilistic. The protagonist knows every unbearable truth and still finds some small defiant reason to continue. The key may be something the Architects failed to account for in their cold calculations - something fundamentally human that their experiment was never designed to measure. Love, resistance, the mother-daughter bond itself. The experiment was designed to study degradation and collapse but the protagonist represents something the data cannot capture. She cannot change their circumstances but she changes something within them. The darkness earns the flicker of light. It is not rescue. It is not resolution. It is quiet defiance.

## Tone and Influences
Very dark. Inspired by the quiet civilizational dissolution, unreliable reality, and two-woman dynamic of Midnight Sun (Twilight Zone) and the feeling of familiar environment becoming alien and unrecognizable from Masks (TNG). Not a possession story. The horror lives in perception, inherited powerlessness, and suppressed truth rather than supernatural agency.
