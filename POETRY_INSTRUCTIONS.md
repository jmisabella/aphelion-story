## Overview and Purpose:
Create a collection of original poems inspired by the dark sci-fi story "Aphelion." This is a generation-ship tale of quiet institutional dread, gradual psychological erosion, and existential isolation in a closed system designed for observation and controlled collapse. The mood to evoke: haunting introspection, subtle unease, the weight of inherited deception, the cold indifference of engineered systems, and small acts of human defiance against inevitable decay. Poems should be thought-provoking and intellectually engaging for mature audiences, exploring themes like preservation vs. erasure, the unrecordable bonds between people, and the horror of truths hidden in plain sight. Avoid overly literal retellings of the story's plot (e.g., no direct references to characters like Calla or Lisel, or specific events like the ghost file); favor abstraction, metaphor, and subtlety to capture the essence poetically. Poems will be used in a TTS app mode, so ensure they read well aloud with natural rhythm and pacing.

## File and Directory Structure:

Save each poem as a separate .txt file in the poems/ directory.

Name files sequentially (e.g., aphelion_poem_01.txt, aphelion_poem_02.txt, etc.).
Each file should contain only the poem text (no titles, headers, or footnotes—titles can be optional and simple if they enhance TTS flow, but keep them minimal).

## Number of Poems:

Generate 10-12 poems in total. Aim for 10 as the baseline, adding up to 2 more if inspiration yields high-quality pieces that fit the mood without repetition. This provides variety for random TTS playback while aligning with the story's 8 chapters.
Style and Form Guidelines:

## Rhyming vs. Free Verse Mix: 
Roughly 50-60% rhyming poems, 40-50% free verse. Don't force the balance—go with what feels natural for each poem's content and mood. If a poem starts as free verse but naturally develops rhythmic elements, let it evolve.

#### Rhyming Poems: 
Use subtle, non-forced rhymes (e.g., internal or slant rhymes for a modern feel). Occasionally skip an expected rhyme for emphasis or unease (e.g., to mimic the story's themes of disruption or absence). Avoid sing-song patterns; aim for a haunting, incantatory quality that suits TTS reading.

#### Free Verse Poems: 
Embrace fragmentation, enjambment, and irregular line lengths for atmospheric tension. It's fine (and encouraged) if occasional lines rhyme inconsistently or echo sounds from earlier lines, adding subtle musicality without structure.

## Mood and Tone: 
Dark, introspective, and subtly ominous, reflecting "Aphelion's" themes of existential isolation, engineered deception, and quiet resistance. Evoke feelings of vast emptiness, mechanical indifference, eroded certainty, and the persistence of human connection amid collapse. Make them thought-provoking for mature readers—probe philosophical undercurrents like what endures when truth is obscured, or the horror of living in a system built to observe your dissolution. Avoid optimism or resolution; lean into ambiguity and lingering dread.

## Length and TTS Considerations: 
Each poem should take roughly 3-6 minutes when read aloud by a normal-speed TTS system voice (e.g., British or Australian accents at standard pace). Test approximate timing by word count (aim for 300-600 words, but prioritize natural flow over strict limits). Use line breaks, pauses (via punctuation), and rhythm to enhance TTS delivery—shorter lines for tension, longer for introspection. It's okay if 1-2 poems fall outside this range if the content demands it (e.g., a shorter, punchier piece for emphasis).

## Content Guidelines:
Focus on abstraction and subtlety: Use metaphors drawn from the story's essence (e.g., echoes in empty corridors, shadows of absent signals, hands holding against an invisible pull) without direct plot spoilers or literal references. Explore universal extensions like the void's patience, hidden architectures in everyday words, or bonds that outlast designed obsolescence.

Vary perspectives and imagery: Some poems from a detached observer's view (like the archive), others intimate (like a fading connection), others cosmic (the ship's indifferent path). Ensure diversity to avoid repetition across the collection.

Mature Audience Fit: Incorporate intellectual depth, subtle horror, and philosophical nuance suitable for adults—evoke unease through implication rather than graphic content.

## Generation Process:
Generate poems one at a time or in small batches, reviewing each for mood alignment, natural form, and TTS flow.
After completing the set, ensure the overall balance (rhyming/free verse) isn't too lopsided—if it skews heavily one way, revise 1-2 to rebalance.
Final Check: Read each aloud (or simulate TTS) to confirm 3-6 minute timing and evocative delivery.
